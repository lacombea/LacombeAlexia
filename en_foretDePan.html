Abstract

Pan's Forest is a serious musical action adventure game. It allows the player to learn to recognize the gaps between harmonic progressions. To do so, the player embodies a musician who must calm the animals of the forest by playing the chord that corresponds to that which the animal sings.

Video Gameplay

Play

Play with the WASD (qwerty) or ZQSD (azerty) keys to move, E to use the radar and R to calm the animals.

Gameplay and mechanics

The player must find the animals hidden in the forest and then calm them down.
To do so, he can sing: his song is harmonic 1 and the nearest animal responds to him by singing his own harmonic. The player can thus identify where the animal is located because the sound is spatialized and an arrow indicates the direction of the animal. In addition, he will be able to recognize the harmonic associated with the animal thanks to the sound.
Once the animal is nearby, the player must calm it down by doing a combo of the number corresponding to the animal's harmonic.

My role

I participated in the development and programming of this game. Here are the main tasks I took care of during the project:

Implementation of the 3Cs (Camera, Character, Controller)

I set up the inputs for this project with Unity's "New Inputs Manager" and took care of setting up the Top-down camera using Cinemachine.

Implemented mechanic to calm animals

I took care of setting up the combo to calm the animals: for a few seconds the player can press the "calm" button and increase the combo to the desired value. If it goes above 7, which is the maximum difference between the harmonics, the combo goes back to 1. I realized the display of this one on the screen and also the sound and visual feedback of this one.
