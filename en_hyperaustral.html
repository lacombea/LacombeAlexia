Abstract

Hyperaustral is a top-down 2D management game, where the player can see, control and organize everything that takes place in the huge ark plane he has. Over time, he will have to keep his crew alive against the obstacles of the game by ordering his passengers to perform various tasks and solving choices on textual interfaces. Gameplay and mechanics

Heart of the game

The heart of the game is to assign passengers to tasks in each of the rooms of the ark-plane. This action is performed a significant number of times by the player so must be simple and intuitive to perform. The controls and an adapted UI will allow the player to choose the passengers to be assigned quickly. This is the main mechanic and it allows the player to react to the different events that will punctuate the game.

Gameplay Brick

Global Resource Management

The player will have to manage several resources in order to finish the game with the most passengers alive. These resources will be global to the plane and will empty during the game but can be produced by the passengers. There's fuel, aircraft health, "raw" food, and cooked food. The player will be able to define his own resource management strategy in order to prepare himself to better react to events.

  • Global resources

Management of passenger needs

Each passenger has needs that must be met to keep them alive and avoid overflows. There is morale, health, hunger and fatigue. The lower a passenger's needs gauges are, the less productive and focused they will be in their tasks.

  • Passenger needs

Events

During the game, multiple events occur randomly. They can have both positive and negative consequences. For some items, a choice will be given to players to choose their reaction.

My role

I participated in the development and programming of this game. Here are the main tasks I took care of during the project:

Implementation of passenger assignment mechanics

I implemented the mechanics to assign passengers to rooms and activities within the Notos ark-plane.
To do this I first designed the structure of the rooms and activities: the rooms are represented by scripts which reference activities as well as their maximum capacity and the activities which are scriptable objects which reference the resources consumed and/or produced with their rate as well as the needs of the passengers satisfied and/or exhausted and their rate.

  • Scriptable object of an activity

Implementation of menus and HUD

I implemented the navigation between the different menus and scene of our game as well as the HUD following the ideas designed by one of the artists of the team. It was also necessary to ensure that all the systems of the other players were affected by the gear changes and to adapt them if necessary.

  • Main Menu

Implementation of backups

We wanted to leave open the possibility of being able to save and resume the game later. So I took care of setting up the backup system using a Json file. It was thus necessary to save the passengers, the past events as well as the game time, the rest of the information of the game can thus be deduced. For example, the current chapter could be deducted by the game time.

Implementation of time management

An important mechanic of management games being the flow of time: paused, normal or accelerated, I took care of implementing all this system in order to make the player fully in control of his ship.
